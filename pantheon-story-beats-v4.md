# The Dance of Light and Dark

## How the Stars Were Born

*A myth of the Pantheon, as told by the Keepers of the Twilight - those who tend the hour between, when the sky remembers how it learned to blend*

---

## Act 1: The First Imbalance

### The Original Heartbeat

- Before stars, sunrises, sunsets, shadows, or twilight
- Light and Darkness trade the sky in binary - ON/OFF, the original heartbeat, the swell of tides. The one that every heartbeat since has been an echo of
- No gradients, no blending, no between. No dawn. No dusk. Just the switch
- In the light, things are seen. In the dark, things rest. Neither questions this. You do not question breathing
- The cosmos is nearly empty - not empty like a room but empty like a blank page before the first word. Endless, echoing, exhilarating room
- Chaos finds two friends. Separately. This is the hinge on which the whole story turns
- **Chaos and Creation**: Chaos manifests feminine - the spark to Creation's flame. The creative best friend who shows up crackling with ideas. "Come ON, you have to see this." The muse to the maker. They spiral upward together in a helix of creative energy so intoxicating the other gods can feel it like heat from a furnace
- **Chaos and Destruction**: Chaos manifests masculine - the wild buddy. Confident, easy, grinning. "Dude, put down the scalpel, when was the last time you just DID something without a risk assessment?" Gives the careful god permission to stop being the surgeon for five minutes. Destruction gives Chaos ballast - the steadiness to lean against when the feelings are too many
- **Chaos does not know they do this.** The mirror does not see itself change shape. Same love, same intensity inside. Different exterior. They've never stopped long enough to notice
- **Creation and Destruction are NOT friends** - halting, awkward, can barely speak directly. Connected only through Chaos. The handoff happens through gravity, not conversation. Make. Spark. Clear. The heartbeat within the heartbeat
- The jealousy simmers from the start:
  - Creation sees Chaos arrive at Destruction's side as someone ELSE. Masculine. Easy. Laughing low. Leaning. Resting. She wants that version. Her nature pulls the manic muse from Chaos automatically and she can't get the calm one
  - Destruction sees Creation celebrated by the entire cosmos - Light examines her work, Order sorts it, Growth nurtures it. Everyone notices making. Nobody applauds the janitor. Nobody looks at an empty field and says "that was beautiful." Chaos is the ONLY god who celebrates his endings - "go bigger, that was INCREDIBLE, do it again!" The only applause he has ever received. And Creation gets everyone else's attention AND Chaos's ecstatic version
- **Light** observes - compulsively examining everything new. Believes her seeing is an act of love. Cannot understand why total illumination could feel like surveillance. Invites Order along. "Come see this"
- **Order** manages fine early - small elegant system, the engineer satisfied when the machine runs clean. Anxiety hums in the background but it's manageable
- **Growth** tends patiently - the gardener who knows the right season. Active patience. Tending, watching, knowing the right moment to push
- **Decay** sits in his garden. Everything returns eventually. No rush
- **Unity** weaves bonds with genuine warmth. Belonging without suffocation
- **Division** draws clean lines. Boundaries without isolation
- **Eternity** preserves what's made. The archive grows slowly, carefully
- **Oblivion** gently erases what needs forgetting. The void is his body, not his home. Merciful and quiet
- **Darkness** holds the space between. Content in solitude. Present without demanding

### Too Much of Everything

- Chaos fuels Creation - more ideas, wilder. The mania crosses from "I want to make" to "I CANNOT STOP making" without Creation noticing. You cannot see stars at noon - Chaos burns too bright beside her for her to see her own exhaustion
- Chaos fuels Destruction - "let go, stop overthinking, just swing." Endings get bigger, less careful. The scalpel widening. The careful god allowing himself the intoxicating permission to just ACT
- Creation notices Destruction's endings getting bigger. The jealousy festers - she makes FASTER to demand Chaos's full attention, flooding the field competitively. If Chaos is going to fuel Destruction's endings, she will make so MUCH that the endings can't keep up
- Destruction notices Creation's output increasing specifically after Chaos visits him - reads it as punishment through productivity. The flood of half-made things as a message: you don't get to have Chaos
- Chaos at the center feels tension in both directions, understands none of it, responds by doing MORE. More is the only tool Chaos owns
- **Order's** irritation builds - the senior engineer watching junior developers push unreviewed code to production. "This doesn't WORK. What is it FOR?" Grinding, professional fury. Turns to Division for support
- **Division** deflects with sarcasm. "Obviously it's unsustainable. I've been saying this since -" / "Then HELP me." / "I don't sort. I separate. Sort your own shelves." Division pulls away. Order frustrated - reaching for an ally who understands edges, getting snark
- Order's administrative static - the noise of overtaxed systems - radiates into the void. Low-frequency. Constant. Wearing
- **Growth** loses her natural patience. Starts forcing things to bloom before they root. The tumor. The coach screaming at athletes to be MORE. Her overexpression is specifically the LOSS of her patience, replaced by hungry urgency
- **Unity's** warmth curdles into clinginess. Bonds fraying. Desperation replacing connection. Pulling everyone closer as they pull away
- **Oblivion** begins to fill - extracted from by Creation (fistfuls of potential yanked from his body), dumped into by Destruction (ended things discarded carelessly), vibrated by Order's static (sandpaper on exposed nerve for an autistic god). The void that is his body starts to ache. His silence becomes noisy

### The Question That Had No Answer

- Chaos arrives at Creation's side still carrying the residue of Destruction's company - manifested feminine but something settled about them. The ghost of the masculine ease still clinging
- Creation confronts: "Why does he get THAT version of you? The one that laughs. The one that leans. The one that actually RESTS."
- Chaos genuinely bewildered. "What VERSION? I'm just ME." The mirror doesn't see itself change shape
- Creation's grief underneath the jealousy: "Why can't you be like that with ME?" Knowing her nature - her loud, manic, cannot-stop-making nature - is the reason Chaos becomes calmer in someone else's presence
- Not a villain's choice. A person asking a question that has no good answer
- **Chaos's BPD cascade** - not gradual shifts. Channel changes. Each emotion TOTAL, replacing the last instantly. Chaos is not choosing these. They are being THROWN:
  - **Confusion** - genuine bewilderment. "What did I DO? Why can't we all be friends? I don't UNDERSTAND"
  - **Grief** - instant switch. Floor-level sobbing. "I'm sorry, I don't know what I did wrong." Apologizing for existing
  - **Rage** - instant switch. Sobbing stops like a door slamming. "I gave you EVERYTHING and THIS is what I get?"
  - **Reactive flight** - hot, impulsive. "FINE. I'm going to go hang out with my OTHER friend and we're going to have the BEST time and you can sit here ALONE." Not cold spite - the rage finding a direction. Not thinking about consequences. Thinking about making a point
- Chaos goes to Destruction

### The Quiet Three

- At the edges of everything, the three quietest gods notice what the louder gods can't see
- Darkness has been watching Oblivion for a while now. The void is full. The gentle god is overwhelmed. Darkness can feel it the way you feel a change in pressure before a storm
- Decay arrives. Of course he does - the healthiest bond in the pantheon. Sits beside Darkness the way he always does. Comfortable silence. No performance
- Decay, dry: "This won't hold." He doesn't mean the cosmos. He means the balance. He can feel things starting to rot that shouldn't be rotting yet - things returning to him carrying toxicity, the corrosive byproduct of careless endings
- Oblivion nearby. Losing edges. Losing words. The three quietest gods at the first tremors of something none of the louder gods have noticed yet
- The stable center. Feeling the ground shift beneath them

## Act 2: The Cosmic Cascade

### Chaos & Destruction

- Chaos arrives at Destruction masculine, hot, jagged - the wild buddy energy curdled into "let's GO, let's tear it UP"
- For Destruction: being CHOSEN. Chaos came to HIM. Over Creation. First time he's ever been someone's first choice. More intoxicating than any permission Chaos ever gave him. Dissolves every guardrail "the way a flood dissolves a dam built for rain"
- Together they tear through the cosmos in waves. First the half-finished things. Then the working things. Then the structures holding everything together
- Destruction ends Creation's things specifically - the half-formed wonders from the partnership he was jealous of. Personal thoroughness. The janitor finally getting to decide what goes
- Chaos cheers destruction the way they cheered creation - same volume, same intensity. Can't tell the difference between the fire that warms and the fire that burns
- Creation watches what her jealousy started. Not the rampage itself - but the question. The match that lit the fire. Manic to depressive in a single moment. The bird hitting glass. Flat, grey, heavy. The guilt worse than exhaustion because the crash gives her clarity - she can trace every broken thing back to herself. To the jealousy. To the question with no good answer. The people-pleaser screaming to fix it, perform, become what they need. But she can't move. The maker stops

### The Gods Break

- **Order** shatters - not from volume but from Chaos's RANDOMNESS. Anti-pattern. The opposite of pattern. Reaching for structure, gripping smoke. Professional irritation becomes grinding fury becomes the engineer staring at a codebase where every function rewrites itself. Then Destruction - already past his guardrails - tears apart Order's OWN SYSTEM. Not the cosmos. Order's personal architecture. The categories. The sorting. The careful elegant machine. Order doesn't scream. Armor of competence holds. Hands sorting air that no longer contains structure. "He always seems so together." This is the sentence that should break your heart
- **Growth** freezes. Nothing left to push. The patient gardener has been screaming "GROW" so long she forgot how to tend. Stares at scorched earth. Hands still reaching for things to grow, closing on nothing
- **Unity's** web snaps - every bond simultaneously. The network she wove desperately gives way like a net under too much weight. Collapses around what remains. The hug that won't let go. Crushing what she's trying to save. The desperation of someone who defines herself through connection losing every connection at once
- **Division** goes silent. The narcissistic mask ready: "I told you so. I told you ALL so." But the words die in his throat. Being right about isolation doesn't feel like winning when the isolation is real. When the walls he built to protect himself are the same walls that kept him from helping anyone. He could have been a boundary. Instead he was a wall. Afraid behind his own architecture. For Division, silence is the most terrifying state
- The system fails god by god

### The Edge of Everything

- It didn't start with the rampage. It started ages ago
- Creation pulling raw material from Oblivion's body with increasing frenzy. Each extraction a small violation he couldn't name
- Destruction dumping ended things into Oblivion's body with decreasing care. Not composting. Dumping
- Order's static vibrating through him constantly. Sandpaper on exposed nerve
- Now Chaos's entropy flooding the last quiet spaces
- The void that is his body losing coherence. Edges blurring. Pieces drifting. Words dissolving before they form. The autistic god in total sensory overload - the world too loud and too bright and too much and too EVERYTHING
- Darkness already with him. Has been with him. Wraps the deep dark around them both
- Oblivion's power LEAKS - the field of forgetting expanding beyond his control. Not an attack. Not a choice. The overflow of a god who has absorbed too much
- Black holes form. Places where pain pools and reality develops permanent wounds. Erasure - light, gravity, matter, sound swallowed. The mortal world would later call them physics. Scars on the gentlest god in the pantheon
- Every god feels the flickering. Memories dimming. Identities unclear. Growth feels a goal shimmer. Unity feels a bond go transparent. Division feels a piece of himself becoming unclear - the line between Division and not-Division drawn in pencil, being gently erased
- Eternity's archive fogs. THE existential threat: not destruction but erasure. Even the permanent record dissolving
- The cosmos holds its breath
- Then **Eternity stands up**. The mountain deciding to walk. Unprecedented. His OWN domain threatened - things he has held for ages dissolving. Drops the weary patience entirely. Clear-eyed, urgent, afraid. "Even I won't remember what we lose." Throws Order the problem "the way you throw a rope to someone drowning. Not gently. Accurately." Needs Order to build a new system. Needs Darkness to negotiate with Light. When the immovable moves, everyone notices. The turning point

### The Quiet Three

- Darkness holding Oblivion at the edge of everything. The deep dark wrapped around them both. Black holes still forming at the edges
- Decay arrives. Of course he does. Sits at the exact distance. Not close enough to crowd, not far enough to abandon
- The three quietest gods at the bottom of everything. Taking stock of the damage
- Darkness, flat: "He's losing pieces. The whole thing is losing pieces." Decay, dry: "Half of what broke wasn't holding together properly anyway. I'd been eyeing some of it for composting"
- They don't fix. They assess. The stable center of the pantheon, doing patient work while the cosmos burns
- Decay: "She'll come to you. She always does when it gets too bright"
- He means Creation. He's right

## Act 3: The Birth of Twilight

### Creation Finds Darkness

- She arrives on her own. After the crash. After the guilt has settled from crushing weight into something she can carry. Goes to Darkness the way a migraine sufferer pulls the blinds. Not choosing. Needing
- Darkness not waiting - just present in the spaces where others are not. The place you arrive when everywhere else has failed
- "I can't make anything." / "Then don't."
- "I can't even rest. Everywhere I go it's so bright. Everyone needs me to make things. Light needs things to see. Order needs things to sort. Chaos needs things to -" / "Not here. Nobody's watching. Nobody needs anything."
- She sits. Sleeps. Darkness does nothing. The most generous thing you can offer a depleted person: the absence of demand. Not help. Not comfort. Not even kindness. Just space with no expectations attached
- Oblivion, nearby, feels the pressure ease by a fraction. Creation is no longer extracting. Just resting. The void can breathe
- She sleeps for a long time. Darkness does not check on her. Does not move. Present the way a mountain is present

### Stars and Shadows

- While Creation sleeps in his shadow, Darkness absorbs the shape of the problem
- The binary is broken. ON/OFF doesn't work. The cosmos needs in-between states - transition, blending, the space where light and dark coexist
- Fixing it means letting Light into his dark. Permanently. His solitude compromised forever. "Imagine asking the ocean to accept a permanent island"
- Looks at Oblivion. Looks at Creation sleeping. Doesn't deliberate. Lets the answer settle like sediment. "My comfort is not worth more than his peace."
- CROSSES HIS OWN DOMAIN to reach Light. The bravest act. An introvert forcing himself to initiate. Every step a small violence against his own architecture
- Darkness speaks in flat logistics. No temperature. "She's with me. She burned out. Pieces of you. Small ones. In my dark."
- Light quiet - the extraordinary act of NOT examining. Just hearing
- "I didn't know seeing could hurt someone." / "It's what you are. You couldn't know."
- "Is she alright?" / "She will be."
- "I don't understand why shadow helps." / "You don't have to."
- Darkness proposes: stop trading in binary. Stop the switch. Let the light fade into dark and the dark fade into light. Share the sky
- Light agrees. Not because she understands. Because she trusts that Darkness is asking for the right reason. The first compromise between the two oldest forces in existence
- Light allows some of herself into Darkness's domain. Softly. Partially. Not the full blinding illumination - just enough to see shapes
- Creation wakes. The want is different from the compulsion. Softer. A desire, not a drive. A reaching-toward, not a being-pushed
- "I think I want to make something." / "Then make something."
- There is light in the dark now. Soft, borrowed, partial. Enough to see by. Not enough to feel examined. The negotiation changed her workshop without her knowing
- She discovers: making in the dark is BETTER. No audience. No Light examining things before they're done. No pressure to perform. She can take her time. Shape by feel. Finish things before showing them
- Reaches into the void GENTLY - dipping fingertips, not grabbing fistfuls. Treating Oblivion's body with the care nobody showed during the crisis
- Shapes by feel. Destruction's approach - weight, consideration, "what are you trying to become?" Learning his language through practice, not conversation
- A sphere. Smooth. Cool. Heavy with intention. The first thing she ever made that was finished before she showed it to anyone
- Darkness thins the local shadow - just a sliver, just enough
- The sphere gathers the scattered light and gives it back as a glow. Soft. Reflected. Secondhand warmth. She made the moon. Not on purpose. By feel. With care
- Darkness does not comment. Commenting would be evaluation
- The moon changes everything: she can SEE in the dark now. Really see. Enough to work by. Not enough to be judged by. The tool that makes the dark workshop permanent
- Then more. Smaller. Scattered. Stars - tiny points placed throughout Darkness's domain. Light living inside the dark. Not replacing it. Inhabiting it
- The dark becomes the workshop. The light becomes the gallery. This is Creation's long-term balance - not a crisis recovery but a permanent change. Things made in the dark, with care, shown only when ready
- When she's ready - truly ready, not compulsively - she comes out of the dark and brings her finished things into the light. Moon. Stars. Complete. Careful
- When Light shines on Creation's objects, shadows form below. Darkness persisting in the day. Nobody designed this. It just happens - the natural consequence of putting things between light and ground. The most elegant solutions aren't engineered. They emerge
- Light perceives shapes in the dark for the first time through the moon's reflected glow. Outlines, not details. Agonizing AND a relief. Something adjacent to rest. Partial seeing as a gift she didn't know she needed
- Light notices shadows in her own domain. Darkness present in HER light. Not an invasion. A natural consequence. A collaboration nobody designed
- The stars carry a lifecycle that involves the whole wheel: Creation makes them. Eternity sustains them - they burn for ages. Growth feeds their fire. Destruction ends them - supernovae, the most spectacular endings in the cosmos. The most beautiful thing Destruction has ever done. Some collapse inward, feeding the black holes where Oblivion's old wounds still ache. The cycle written in the sky
- **Order** sees things moving between domains. Creation making in the dark. Finished things emerging into the light. Moon reflecting. Stars inhabiting. Shadows persisting. The answer isn't just sharing the sky. It's a CYCLE. A rotation
- Designs the dawn - incremental, golden, seamless. Light arriving gradually instead of all at once
- Designs the dusk - more colors than dawn. "Yielding is harder than arriving, and the harder thing deserved more beauty"
- Designs the twilight - the impossible hour between. The gradient that proves light and dark can coexist
- The original heartbeat becomes the dance. Not ON/OFF anymore - a waltz. Light and Darkness flowing into each other and back again
- The compulsion that was his prison becomes his gift. Built by anxious hands. Works perfectly

### The First Sunset

- While the mending happens, Chaos is moving. Not crashing - Chaos doesn't crash. Already laughing somewhere. Already fully present in a new moment as if the last age never happened
- But one by one, the interactions end. Everyone is busy mending. Creation is in the dark. Destruction has gone quiet. Order is building. Everyone is somewhere else
- Chaos is standing in the middle of it. Still buzzing. Nobody to mirror. No one to become
- The terrifying question for a god whose form is always defined by who they're with: without someone to reflect, what is Chaos? What shape does the water take when nothing holds it?
- Finds Darkness. Not on purpose. By running out of everywhere else
- Darkness doesn't need Chaos to be anything. The absence of demand
- For the first time, Chaos doesn't perform a gender or an energy or a role. No mirror. No reflection. Just whatever they are underneath all the presentations. Not crashed. Not broken. Just... themselves. They don't know what that looks like. That's the point
- Darkness doesn't comment. He's just there
- This is not a crash. This is Chaos meeting Chaos. The version that exists when no one else is in the room
- Chaos emerges changed. Not healed - changed. Knowing what they are without a mirror. Something underneath the buzzing that wasn't there before
- Sees Order's gradients in motion. Dawn and dusk. Stars and moon in the sky. Recognizes something: breaking apart can be beautiful. Breaking apart can be a GIFT
- During the transition - Order's careful gradient from light to dark - Chaos touches the light. Not with rage. Not with entropy. With the thing they learned in the dark: that what you are when no one's watching is still real, still yours, still worth something
- The light SPLITS. White light shatters into spectrum. Every color hidden inside the unity of white suddenly visible, distinct, radiant. Not disorder. Revelation. The colors were always there. Chaos just showed what was hidden in something everyone thought they understood
- Every sunset different because Chaos can't do the same thing twice. Order's framework holding the wild energy. Structure and entropy collaborating for the first time
- Three gods look at the sunset and see their own truth:
  - **Unity** sees colors flowing together, blending at the edges, a spectrum MORE beautiful because it's whole. "See? They belong together"
  - **Division** sees each color distinct, with its own identity, necessary BECAUSE it's different. "See? They matter because they're distinct"
  - **Order** finally has proof for the thing he could never articulate: "THIS. This is the SAME ACT." The bridge with evidence at last
- For the first time, Unity and Division look at the same thing and are both right. The colors blend AND they're distinct. The boundaries are real AND the connection is real. Neither has to lose
- Creation sees Chaos's gift clearly - not the manic spark but the ability to reveal hidden beauty by breaking apart. Which is also what Destruction does. Breaking and making aren't opposites. The same act from different angles
- Destruction sees the same energy from the rampage producing THIS. The regret: he could have been chosen AND stayed careful. The wild energy could have been held instead of unleashed
- Chaos, who did this accidentally, who never does anything on purpose, looks at the sky: "Oh. That's pretty."
- The most chaotic god accidentally creating the most peace. Never on purpose. That's not how Chaos works

### The New Balance

- **Creation**: makes in the dark, shows in the light. Covenant with Order - "when they're ready, not before." The compulsive flood becomes a sustainable craft
- **Order**: the engineer given a worthy system. Built the rotation. Then Chaos filled it with color and proved the thing he'd been telling Unity and Division for ages. Anxious hands. Works perfectly. Even the parts he didn't build
- **Destruction**: returns to careful endings. Stars die in his hands and it's the most spectacular thing in the sky. The thread between him and Creation - thin, fragile, not friendship. Something more complicated. Enough
- **Growth**: rediscovers patience. Slowing down IS growth. The hardest kind
- **Unity**: looks at the sunset and sees colors belonging together. Fewer bonds, stronger. Doesn't need Division to be wrong
- **Division**: looks at the same sunset and sees colors distinct. The cosmos proved boundaries matter AND coexist with connection. Self-doubt eases one degree. For Division, an ocean
- **Eternity**: sustains the stars. They burn for ages under his care. The mountain sat back down but remembers standing
- **Decay**: "They decompose better when made with care. It's nicer when it returns tidy"
- **Oblivion**: heals as the system heals. Creation treats the void gently now. Destruction is careful with endings. Order's static has quieted. He pulls himself inward - the way a wounded animal uncurls when the danger passes. The black holes remain - permanent, ominous, active. Wounds that will never close. But no new ones form. The kindness was always there. It just needed room to breathe
- **Darkness**: solitude compromised but Oblivion is safe. Creation in his shadow. Chaos in his quiet. Stars in his sky. The cost was worth the cost
- **Light**: partial seeing through the moon. Shadows in her domain. Her light split into colors she didn't know she carried. Something adjacent to rest
- **Chaos**: the god who met themselves in the dark. Accidentally painted the sunset. The flinch from Creation is softer now. The stillness from Destruction is warmer. Maybe the sunset is doing the work for them slowly, without anyone forcing it

**The Mortal World:**
- Poets find words shaped in the dark. Painters sketch in shadow. Musicians compose in silence
- Children make things in secret - the most precious things
- Every evening: twilight. Gold to amber to rose to violet. No two sunsets the same
- Every morning: dawn. The proof the blending works both ways
- Every night: the moon. Enough light to work by. Not enough to be judged by
- Stars that burn for ages and die in spectacular explosions. Some collapse into black holes - still swallowing, still reminding
- Shadows in the day. Rainbows after storms. The sky breathing in gradients

**The Secrets the Sky Keeps:**
1. The greatest making happens in the dark, by borrowed light, with patience
2. The dark was never empty - it was resting
3. Creation made the moon and stars by feel, with Destruction's care. The gentlest making
4. Order turned the heartbeat into a dance. Anxious hands. Works perfectly
5. Destruction's silence was a wound. Being chosen overrode every guardrail. But stars die in his hands and it is the most spectacular thing in the sky
6. Chaos met themselves in the dark. Then shattered the light into colors nobody knew were there. The most accidental gift
7. Oblivion suffered most, spoke least. Black holes are his wounds. The sky is mostly dark because Darkness promised. The void healed because the wheel learned to stop hurting him
8. Decay sat with Darkness while the world burned. Mildly declined to leave
9. Eternity stood up. The god who never moved was moved. The rarest thing
10. The sunset proved Unity and Division right at the same time. Order wept
11. Shadows were never designed. They happen because things exist in light
12. Stars are born, endure, and die in glory. Some collapse into the void. The whole cycle overhead every night

### The Quiet Three (Reprise)

- The same three gods. The same quiet. But everything is different
- Darkness, Decay, Oblivion. The stable center, back in balance
- Decay, dry as ever: "You let her put STARS in your sky. You HATE company"
- Darkness says nothing. But he doesn't deny it. Because his solitude is compromised but Oblivion is safe and Creation visits and the cost was worth the cost
- Decay can see it. The quiet happiness Darkness would never say out loud. Decay won't let him off the hook. That's what best friends are for
- Oblivion is back to quiet. Back to the size of nothing. The black holes still there - permanent, ominous - but no new ones. The void is mostly void again. Stars are visitors, not intrusions
- Creation sneaks into the dark to make things. Not crashing this time. Choosing it. The workshop, not the hiding place. Darkness pretends not to notice. Decay pretends not to notice Darkness pretending not to notice
- Late at night, Chaos finds them. Thanks Darkness. Quietly. A single form, unhurried
- Darkness, flat: "She still needs your fire. The dark can hold her. It can't make her spark"
- The real grin. Relief. Still needed. Not replaced
- Different gifts. They can't be given at the same time. This is not a flaw in the design. It IS the design
- Darkness wraps the deep dark around Oblivion one more time. The black beyond the furthest star
- "The dark will always be mostly yours. The void will always be mostly void. The black holes are yours. The sky will always be mostly night."
- "Promise?"
- "Promise."

- Candles lit - one bright, one dark. Makers stand in the hour between
- "Go make something in the dark, dear one. The moon will give you just enough light."
- "Finish what you start. End what needs ending. Let the rest be void."
- "The twilight is waiting for you. It was built by anxious hands. It works perfectly."
